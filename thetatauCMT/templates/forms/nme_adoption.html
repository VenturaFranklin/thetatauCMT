After a multi-year effort that included education professionals interviewing students at the 2012 Convention in Boston, and phone interviews with other students at their campuses, Theta Tau, working with RISE Partnerships, published the New Member Education Manual in Fall 2013.

This Manual comes in three versions:
Version NameDescriptionDownload Link
Basic suggested for struggling chapters and new colonies. It also provides a basis for replacing a chapter’s problematic pledge education practices with a safe, creative, and supportive program. http://thetatau.org/Websites/thetatauhq/images/Member_Education_Basic_Combined.pdf
Non-traditional suggested for commuter schools or campuses with a number of part-time, night-time, and returning students. It suggests ways to organize activities/events when campus space is not readily available. http://thetatau.org/Websites/thetatauhq/images/Member_Education_Non_Traditional_Combined.pdf
Standard suggested for chapters that are healthy, and on campuses that have an active, supportive, and that operate in a traditional manner with support of the IFC/RSO office. It also serves to filter out unhealthy practices.

The Prospective New Member Workbook (the document given to prospective new members) for each module is found here http://thetatau.org/member-education


Over the past few years, Theta Tau chapters and members have repeatedly been harassed, investigated, and punished by school administrators even for activities that chapter members thought were innocent or fun. In some instances, accusations against chapters were later proven false but still took a serious toll on the chapter’s time and morale. Following a well-documented program such as those contained in these manuals is key to avoiding such troubles and in being able to defend yourselves in the event that accusations do arise.

Finally, chapters not following one of these 3 models for new member education will be assessed a 5% penalty on the annual liability insurance invoices for this school year.
